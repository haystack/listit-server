Summary


Eyebrowse displays interesting and insightful visualizations of your web browsing history to help you discover new websites, gain personal insights and catch a glimpse of what Google knows about you!

note: eyebrowse does not require you to share anything and will not share any activities without your consent. Please see our data handeling policy

if you have any questions please leave a comment


newtab

The 'newtab' page is accessed by clicking File -> New Tab or by pressing command+t on the Mac.
In 'personal' mode the eyebrowse plugin will recommend previously visitied pages and visualize your activity per hour, your top sites and your top domains over the past 24 hours.
In 'social' mode the 'newtab' page will recommend sites to you and provide a ticker of the latest sites. Please help us recommend sites to you by 'following' users you like via their 'profile page' or by adding yourself to groups in your 'settings' page.


lifestream

The lifestream page is accessed by going to File -> New Tab or by pressing command + t on the Mac.
Due to Firefox security restrictions we cannot link to the lifestream page from a url but you can access it and bookmark it from newtab page's footer.


Pulse

This is the eyebrowse pulse page. It gives you an overview of everyone's recent web activity. Once signed in, you can also see this page for just your web activity or your friends web activity.


Pathways

Pathways lets you input any URL and see a ranked list of the most common sites visited before and after, visits to the page by hour of day and day of week, and the top visitors. By clicking on the small graph icon, you may see follow the path from url to url.


profile

A profile page displays the last 15 pages viewed, graphs of overall browsing activity by hour, day and week, and top 10 sites.


plugin

The Eyebrowse Firefox plugin shows you which of your friends spend the most time on the current URL, how many friends have recorded that URL and a ranked list of the most common sites visited before and after. Click the graph icon in the plugin to visit the pathways page. download the plugin
