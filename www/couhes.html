Welcome to the study! We're grateful that you are interested in helping us with our research efforts. Here is some information surrounding the study, what we wish to get out of it, how we will handle the data we collect and the results. If you have any questions, please do not hesitate to contact the researchers via the contact information below.

about the study

This is a study of personal note-taking practice for the purpose of achieving a better understanding people's note taking needs. Our ultimate is to design better tools for helping people keep track of their personal information.

This study represents the second phase of our research project, in which we wish to see how people use a simple note-taking tool that we have developed, called list.it.

participation and withdrawal

Your participation in this study is completely voluntary and you are free to choose whether to be in it or not. If you choose to be in this study, you may subsequently withdraw from it at any time without penalty or consequences of any kind. The investigator may withdraw you from this research if circumstances arise which warrant doing so.

If the investigator determines that you are not fulfilling the requirements of the study, he/she may decide to terminate the study.

procedure

If you volunteer to participate in this study, we would ask you to do the following things:

preparation

You will be installing a note-taking tool on the one or more personal computer(s) you use most often. This tool will require Firefox version 3.0 or above to be installed on each of these computers, which may be obtained from Mozilla.org.

usage

You will then be asked to try to make an explicit effort to frequently use the system over the course of a week to get used to using the system to help manage your notes. During this time, we ask you to keep a diary noting any thoughts that you have regarding the system. The researcher(s) will ask for this feedback via periodic e-mails to you, where you can further elaborate your impressions from using the system.

After this duration, we ask that you try to use the system as naturally as you see fit. If you do not feel the need or desire to continue using the system, you may cease to do so.

During the course that you are using the system, your usage of the system will be logged by the system and periodically automatically report this to the researchers on their server. This information is kept confidential for research purposes only, as described in "Data Handling" below.

potential risks

Risks surrounding this study are minimal. The software exists as a standalone firefox plug-in, and all components will be installed in the user's Firefox profile directory.

As with any computer program, there is a potential risk that the system may glitch and lose some of the information you have recorded into it. While we have installed backup algorithms to make such possibilities less likely, we cannot guarantee that it will not occur. As a result, please take precaution to make copies of critical data that you keep in the system.

data handling and confidentiality

The researchers of the study will not share information that he/she encounters during the interviews, in reviewing the diary, or in processing the application log data, and all notes surrounding your data will be coded by participant number rather than name.

Any information that is obtained in connection with this study and that can be identified with you will remain confidential and will be disclosed only with your permission or as required by law. The software being used may log your usage patterns in order to either improve its interface, contribute to later research statistics, or both. This data will be kept on your hard drive and later transferred to the list.it server (under control of researchers) for analysis â€“ it will not be shared outside the research group.

During analysis, participants will be given a private numeric identifier for their records and data. All data will be identified solely by participant number, so only the head investigator and the participant will have the ability to link data to names. The researcher will retain the number-name correspondences in a secure location separate from the tapes and data.All retained information will be only reported in scientific journals in the form of accumulated statistics and/or the media above.

If other uses of data are contemplated, the researchers will obtain specific consent at that time by contacting the participant.

potential benefits

There will be no direct benefit to the subject for participating in this research. It is plausible that the tool we assign you to use may improve upon your current practices and you may decide to continue its use. The benefit to society will focus on improving information management work for computer users. If successful, the research will be used towards designing new tools that make it easier to manage the large amount of personal information (emails, todos, memories, files) on the computer.
