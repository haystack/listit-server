  • about.it

    At MIT CSAIL we study ways to design better tools to help people cope with information overload and to stay organized.

    Our team consists of Max Van Kleek, Greg Vargas, Michael Bernstein and Katrina Panovich, and our PIs are Prof. David Karger and monica (mc) schraefel.

    list.it is a collaboration with the University of Southampton and the Nokia Research Center, and is supported by the Web Science Research Initiative and the The National Science Foundation.

  • contribute to science
    We need your help! You can help in two ways:
    • Volunteer your notes to science - We need people to try our tools, and let us study how they use them. This gives us permission to construct aggregate statistics about your notes, (e.g., how long they are, what kinds of data they contain), and use these to build smarter tools. We will not disclose your note contents without your explicit permission.
    • Tell us your thoughts - Visit the list.it Google Group and tell us what you think -- what do you want in a tool? Does list.it help? How well does it work for you?
    • File bugs, hack on our code - Visit the list.it Google Code page if you wish to report a bug, or if you are a programmer and want to see the source code.
  • register.it (free backups and sync service)

    If you register, we give you a free account on our server to let you safely back up and synchronize your notes across your computers. Also if you wish to contribute your notes to science, you must register for an account so we have a way to access your usage statistics.

    We will only contact you about critical updates and not disclose your address to anyone outside our research group.

    For our data handling policy please see our FAQ.
