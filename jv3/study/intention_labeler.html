Note intention study

In this exercise you're going to be presented with a bunch of random notes and your job is to guess why they were taken.

For each note, choose a primary category (from the categories below), which you think best reflects the purpose the note was taken. After that, select how likely the note is any of the other categories

Of course you can't read the mind of the note-taker, so just take you best guesses!

If you absolutely haven't a clue what a note means, leave it -no idea-

memory trigger
A note whose purpose is to BRING SOMETHING TO MIND
reference
A piece of information that might be useful later for doing something.
external cognition / brainstorming
notes taken while thinking through something -- writing something down to help you think about it. For example, drafts of papers, outlines of talks you have to give, hard problems you're trying to solve, a plan you're trying to device.
journal/activity log
something that serves as a record that you did or experienced something, or that something happened
posterity
stuff the person wanted to keep "just because", maybe to find some day (for no particular reason except boredom)
